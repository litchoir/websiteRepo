About

Background

The University of Notre Dame Liturgical Choir, founded in 1973 as the Chapel Choir, is composed of approximately 70 male and female singers drawn from all areas of the undergraduate and graduate student body. The choir sings at the Basilica of the Sacred Heart, a French Gothic structure built in 1871 by University founder Fr. Edward Sorin.

Led by director Dr. Andrew McShane and assistant director Jonathan Hehn, the Liturgical Choir performs a repertoire drawn on the diverse traditions of a cappella and accompanied sacred music from the Renaissance through the twentieth century. In recognition of the Liturgical Choir's special ministry to the Notre Dame community, Pope John Paul II extended his apostolic blessing upon the Choir in 1989.

Auditions

General auditions for all University choral ensembles are held at the beginning of each academic year, during Welcome Weekend.

However, at any point during the academic year, students can request an audition by appointment by emailing amcshane@nd.edu. Membership in the Liturgical Choir is open to undergraduate and graduate students of all majors enrolled at Notre Dame, St. Mary's, or Holy Cross.

At your audition, you will be asked to sing a prepared piece or a hymn that you know (Amazing Grace, My Country 'Tis of Thee, Praise to the Lord are popular choices!). We will test your range and your ear, and conclude the audition with a sight-reading exercise. The choral audition takes less than 10 minutes.

Commitments

The Choir's main purpose is to provide musical leadership at the 10 AM (Eastern time) Solemn Mass, which is televised weekly on CatholicTV. CatholicTV can be found on Comcast, Verizon, Full Channel and RCN cable providers in the New England area and is available nationally on Sky Angel IPTV and online at www.CatholicTV.com.

The Liturgical Choir is also responsible for providing liturgical music for services such as: Sunday Vespers Weddings in the Basilica Dedications Ordinations Advent Lessons & Carols Special university liturgies such as Junior Parents' Weekend and the Baccalaureate Mass. Holy Week is of special importance to the Choir, including Palm Sunday, Tenebrae, Good Friday, Easter Vigil, Easter Sunday, and Easter Vespers liturgies.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • addHow many people are in the Liturgical Choir?
    The Notre Dame Liturgical Choir is comprised of around 60-70 members, undergraduate and graduate students from Notre Dame, St. Mary's, and Holy Cross.
  • addWhen does the Liturgical Choir rehearse?
    Tuesdays from 5:00-6:45pm, Thursdays from 6:00-7:45pm, and Sunday mornings at 8:45am with an optional breakfast at 8:30 in the Campus Ministry Kitchen. We walk over after Sunday morning rehearsal to sing at the televised 10am Mass in the Basilica of the Sacred Heart.
  • addHow can I join?
    View auditions information here.
  • addCan I be in both band and choir?
    Yes! In years past, band-choir members will typically leave Tuesday rehearsal 15-20 minutes early and skip Thursday rehearsal for band on football weekends. Be sure to inform the both our director, Andy, and Sam Sanchez about your situation.
  • addWhat are the Liturgical Choir's biggest events?
    We sing at five services during Holy Week and Easter: Tenebrae on Holy Thursday evening, the Good Friday service, the Easter Vigil, the 10am Easter Sunday Mass, and Easter Vespers. We prepare for these throughout most of the spring semester.
    Our other big event is our annual choir tour, which is international every other year.
  • addI'm not Catholic. Can I still sing in the Liturgical Choir?
    Of course! While our focus is assisting with the Catholic Liturgy, we welcome people from all faiths. However, it should be noted that the 10am Basilica Mass is the Liturgical Choir's primary ministry, and conflicts with this time may make the Liturgical Choir a difficult commitment.
  • addWhat else does the Liturgical Choir do besides sing?
    We have various choir events, such as our annual concession stand, Choir Appreciation Week, an S-Choi-R (a fun themed dance with a theme like "Rhyme Without Reason"), social gatherings, and an end-of-year cookout. We go caroling around Christmas, and we eat dinner together as a choir in the dining hall after every rehearsal on Tuesdays and Thursdays and every Mass on Sundays.
  • addCan graduate students join?
    Absolutely. Most of our grad students are in the Masters in Sacred Music program, but we have others from all different departments on campus.
  • addIf I don't go to Notre Dame, or if I live off-campus, how can I come to choir dinner?
    Most (if not all) choir members are more than willing to spare an extra meal swipe for off campus students. St. Mary's and Holy Cross students should be able to get the equivalent of meal swipes. If you run into an issue, contact the choir president, Julian Mancini, or secretary, Michael Kurkowski.
